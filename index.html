Hi, I’m currently a research assistant at the Faculty of Computer Science at the Free University of Bozen-Bolzano (Unibz), Italy, where I received my PhD for research on supporting group decision making process with recommendation techniques. Prior to Unibz, I completed my master’s and bachelor’s degrees at Vietnam National University - HoChiMinh City University of Science and HoChiMinh City University of Education, respectively.
My full first name is Thuy Ngoc, which I use for official documents and publications. In all other situations, such as email exchange or day-to-day communications, I simply go with the shorter version, Ngoc.
Research Interests: (Group) Recommender Systems, User Modeling, and Human Computer Interaction

News

I’ll be joining the Dynamic Decision Making Lab at the Carnegie Mellon University as a postdoctoral fellow this October.

Research Projects

  • A chat-based group recommender system The application domain of this project is point of interests (POIs) recommendations for a group of people. Specifically, I have developed a group recommender system, an Android application, as a working prototype to validate my research hypotheses.
  • Simulation models for group decision making process In this project, we employed simulation experiments to analyze and gain deeper insights into the interplay between group members’ behavior and the performance of group recommendations.
