I am a Human Factors Research Scientist at [Idaho National Laboratory (INL)](https://www.inl.gov) / Battelle Energy Alliance, an Adjunct Professor of Human-Computer Interaction at the [University of Idaho](https://www.uidaho.edu), and the Founder of [Civix Design](https://civixdesign.com) — a human-centered design consultancy focused on civic services and high-stakes systems.

My work sits at the intersection of **human reliability**, **human-computer interaction**, **risk-informed design**, and **artificial intelligence** in safety-critical domains. At INL, I conduct research on human reliability analysis (HRA), advanced reactor operator licensing, AI/ML human factors, and prognostic visualization for nuclear control rooms. I have also developed and delivered training for the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission and received funding from the DOE, DoD, and NRC for applied research in human performance and AI integration.

At the University of Idaho, I teach graduate and undergraduate courses in human factors, accident investigation, and human-computer interaction. Through Civix Design, I apply these same principles to the design of civic digital services, accessibility compliance, and organizational human factors challenges in government and healthcare contexts.

## Research Interests

- Human Reliability Analysis (HRA) and human error modeling
- Human-computer interaction in safety-critical and critical infrastructure systems
- Explainable AI and human factors of AI/ML operator support systems
- Cognitive modeling of dynamic decision-making
- Information visualization and human-system interface design
- Ethics in design and civic design
- Advanced reactor control room design and operator licensing

## Current Work

**Idaho National Laboratory** — Researching the HCI aspects of explainable AI in critical infrastructure; developing NRC staff guidance on advanced reactor operator licensing and remote/autonomous operations human factors; conducting research on human reliability for long-duration spaceflight; supporting research operations leadership in process efficiency and safety.

**University of Idaho** — Teaching PSYC 561 (Human-Computer Interaction) and INDT 466 (Human Performance Field Investigation). Advising student research in human factors and HCI.

**[Civix Design](https://civixdesign.com)** — Providing human-centered design, digital accessibility (ADA/WCAG 2.1 AA), service design, and AI risk evaluation services to public sector and high-stakes private sector clients.

## Background

I hold a PhD in Biological Engineering (human factors focus) and an MS in Experimental Psychology from the University of Idaho, and a BA in Philosophy from Idaho State University. I am a member of the Human Factors and Ergonomics Society (HFES) and the Cognitive Science Society, and I serve as a reviewer for multiple journals and conferences in human factors, nuclear engineering, and ergonomics.

I am based in Idaho Falls, Idaho.
