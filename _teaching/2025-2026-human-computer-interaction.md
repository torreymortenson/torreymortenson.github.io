A graduate-level survey of human-computer interaction theory, research, and practice. The course draws on cognitive psychology, usability engineering, and interaction design to equip students to design, evaluate, and critique digital systems.

**Topics include:** Cognitive foundations of HCI, interaction design principles, usability evaluation methods (heuristic evaluation, user testing, cognitive walkthrough), information architecture, accessibility, and emerging topics in AI-mediated interaction.

**Offered:** Spring 2025, Spring 2026
